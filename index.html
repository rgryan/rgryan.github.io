I am a business analyst, independent research scientist in India and IUCN SSC Canid Specialist Group member. I have experience working with across different signs survey data, big data such as spatial data, genomic data and more recently, financial data.

Dog in the matrix: Envisioning countrywide connectivity conservation for an endangered carnivore
Ryan G. Rodrigues,
Arjun Srivathsa, Divya Vasudev
Journal of Applied Ecology, 2022

Using a data-driven approach, we assessed connectivity of an endangered canid across India using graph, circuit theory and clustering algorithms. We were able to identify ecologically meaningful conservation landscapes; and identify priority populations within each landscape using habitat accessiblity and habitat quality. We superimposed administrative boundaries on our findings to provide conservation recommendations at this management-relevant scale.

The truth about scats and dogs: Next-generation sequencing and spatial capture‚Äìrecapture models offer opportunities for conservation monitoring of an endangered social canid
Arjun Srivathsa, Ryan G. Rodrigues,
Kok Ben Toh, Arun Zachariah, Ryan W. Taylor, Madan K. Oli, Uma Ramakrishnan
Biological Conservation, 2021

We combined genomic tools and spatial population models to, for the first time in India, estimate population sizes of an endangered social carnivore- the dhole. As part of this, we resequenced whole genomes of wild and captive individual dholes to create genetic markers that were then used to identify individuals from non-invasive samples. We then incorporated these genetically identified individuals in spatial population estimation models to estimate population sizes.
